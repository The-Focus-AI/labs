Day One

Hyatt Regency, Miami
April 2026

The Forces Have Moved

Christopher Alexander called patterns frozen resolutions of forces. That’s what these four chapters trace: which forces moved, what refroze, what’s still liquid.

Day One was patterns breaking — the gating function of engineering collapsed, identity scarcity collapsed, Gen-3 SDKs reframed the unit of shipping. The room carried the energy of something ending and something beginning in the same breath.

Day Two was three new patterns hardening — people, companies, and agents all learning agency at the same moment. Every binary the room had been arguing about resolved into a third path.

The question the room used to ask was “will agents replace developers?” By Day Two, that question had evaporated. The actual question was different: how do all three populations — individual practitioners, companies, and autonomous agents — learn to collaborate when each is developing agency in parallel?
